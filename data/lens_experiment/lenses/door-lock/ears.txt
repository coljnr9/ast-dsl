1. **Ubiquitous:** The lock shall provide a mechanical or electronic fastening mechanism to secure an area or object.
2. **Ubiquitous:** The lock shall allow access only to persons possessing the correct physical key, secret information, or authorized electronic token.
3. **State-driven:** While the pins are fell part-way into the bolt (Egyptian wooden pin lock), the lock shall prevent movement of the bolt.
4. **State-driven:** While the driver pins are pushed downwards into the plug (Pin tumbler), the lock shall prevent the plug from rotating.
5. **Event-driven:** When the correct key is inserted into the Egyptian wooden pin lock, the lock shall lift the internal pins out of the bolt holes to allow movement.
6. **Event-driven:** When the bittings of a key align with the shear line, the pin tumbler lock shall allow the cylinder to rotate.
7. **Event-driven:** When a smart key button is pressed, the smart key shall transmit a unique rolling code to the lock.
8. **Event-driven:** When an authorized device sends a cryptographic key via wireless protocol, the smart lock shall execute the lock or unlock instruction.
9. **Event-driven:** When a keycard matches the internal signature of the reader, the keycard lock shall release the fastening device.

11. **Unwanted:** If a lever is not lifted far enough in a double-acting lever lock, then the lock shall prevent the bolt from moving.
12. **Unwanted:** If an unauthorized access attempt occurs, the Chubb detector lock shall frustrate the attempt and indicate interference to the owner.
