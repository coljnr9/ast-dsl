The following rules govern the system of library circulation and the conduct of circulation departments:

1. A library must provide lending services for books and other materials to designated users.
2. The circulation desk serves as the primary public service point for lending and return activities.
3. Libraries must provide resources for the interest, information, and enlightenment of all people in the community they serve.
4. Circulation staff must process the renewal of materials upon request.
5. Borrowers are required to pay overdue fines at the circulation desk.
6. Staff must monitor returned materials for damage.
7. Damaged materials must be routed to specific staff for repair or replacement.
8. Circulation staff must generate and send overdue notices to borrowers.
9. Libraries must collect and track usage statistics, including patron transactions and material checkouts.
10. Personnel must maintain the library stacks by re-shelving materials according to specific call number systems, such as Dewey Decimal or Library of Congress.
11. Circulation staff may provide basic search and reference services.
12. In-depth reference questions must be referred from the circulation desk to a reference librarian or the reference desk.
13. Libraries must safeguard all library use data, including personally identifiable information.
14. A person’s right to use the library shall not be denied or abridged based on origin, age, background, or views.
15. Materials cannot be excluded or removed from the collection due to the origin, background, views, or partisan disapproval of the creator.
16. Circulation staff are authorized to resolve issues concerning inappropriate patron conduct, such as noise levels or cell phone usage.
17. Staff must check in materials upon their return to update the status of the item.
18. Libraries using the Browne Issue System must create and manage borrower pockets.
19. Staff must reconcile financial reports related to fines and department transactions.
20. Professional librarians are not required to staff the circulation desk; support staff may perform these duties.
21. Libraries must make exhibit spaces and meeting rooms available to the public on an equitable basis regardless of user beliefs.
22. Librarians must adhere to state laws regarding the confidentiality and privacy of library use.
23. Circulation staff must possess the physical ability to carry, pull, and lift materials weighing up to 30 pounds.
24. Intellectual freedom policies, as defined by the Library Bill of Rights, must guide all service decisions.
25. Staff must troubleshoot and maintain circulation technology, including scanners, printers, and software.
26. Libraries are prohibited from sharing an individual's library use information with third parties.
27. Privacy and confidentiality rights apply to all people regardless of age or background.
28. Libraries must provide materials that present all points of view on current and historical issues.
29. Circulation supervisors in academic environments are responsible for training student employees.
30. Staff are required to operate and adapt to automated filing and recovery systems.
