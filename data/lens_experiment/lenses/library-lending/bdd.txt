### Happy Paths (Normal Operations)

**Scenario 1: Successful book checkout**
*   **Given** a registered patron and an available book in the library collection
*   **When** the circulation staff processes the lending transaction at the circulation desk
*   **Then** the book's status is updated to "loaned" and a due date is assigned.

**Scenario 2: Successful book return**
*   **Given** a book that is currently checked out to a patron
*   **When** the patron returns the book to the circulation desk
*   **Then** the book is checked back in and removed from the patron's active loans.

**Scenario 3: Renewal of library materials**
*   **Given** a patron has a book currently checked out that is not requested by others
*   **When** the patron requests a renewal at the circulation desk
*   **Then** the system extends the due date for the material.

**Scenario 4: Payment of overdue fines**
*   **Given** a patron has an outstanding fine for a late return
*   **When** the patron pays the fine at the circulation desk
*   **Then** the circulation staff issues a receipt and the fine is cleared from the patron's record.

---

### Precondition Violations (Rejected Operations)

**Scenario 5: Checkout rejected due to patron age (Violation of Bill of Rights)**
*   **Given** a library policy that adheres to the ALA Library Bill of Rights
*   **When** a staff member attempts to deny a checkout based solely on the patron's age
*   **Then** the system should flag this as a violation of the right to use the library regardless of age.

**Scenario 6: Loan rejected for damaged material**
*   **Given** a book is identified as having significant physical damage
*   **When** a checkout is attempted
*   **Then** the circulation staff routes the material for repair and prevents the loan.

---

### State Transitions

**Scenario 7: From "Checked Out" to "In Transit/Shelving"**
*   **Given** a book status is "On Loan"
*   **When** the material is checked in at the desk
*   **Then** the status changes to "To be Shelved" and the staff is prompted to re-shelve it by call number.

**Scenario 8: Transition to "Overdue" status**
*   **Given** a book is on loan and the current date passes the due date slip date
*   **When** the system runs a daily status update
*   **Then** the status of the item changes to "Overdue" and an overdue notice is sent to the borrower.

---

### Edge Cases (Boundaries & Limits)

**Scenario 9: Handling maximum weight for physical tasks**
*   **Given** a circulation staff member is tasked with moving materials
*   **When** a cart or box exceeds 30 pounds
*   **Then** the staff member must seek assistance or divide the load to remain within the 30-pound physical limit requirement.

**Scenario 10: Empty search results for basic reference**
*   **Given** a patron asks for a book that is not in the library’s collection
*   **When** basic search services are performed at the circulation desk
*   **Then** the staff should refer the patron to a reference librarian or suggest an interlibrary loan.

**Scenario 11: Processing a return for an item already checked in**
*   **Given** a book has already been processed as returned
*   **When** the barcode is accidentally scanned again at the desk
*   **Then** the system should display an alert indicating the item is already "In Library."

---

### Query Operations

**Scenario 12: Checking current patron transaction statistics**
*   **Given** the library is currently operating
*   **When** a supervisor requests a report on material checkouts
*   **Then** the circulation staff provides a report reconciling financial and transaction data for that period.

**Scenario 13: Verifying privacy of patron data**
*   **Given** a third party requests information on a specific patron's reading history
*   **When** the librarian reviews the request against privacy laws and ALA policies
*   **Then** the request is denied to protect the confidentiality of the individual’s library use.

---

### Error Handling & Workplace Resolving

**Scenario 14: Circulation technology failure**
*   **Given** the library circulation software or scanner stops responding
*   **When** a checkout is in progress
*   **Then** the circulation staff performs troubleshooting steps or utilizes backup manual filing (e.g., Browne Issue System) to continue service.

**Scenario 15: Resolving inappropriate patron conduct (Noise)**
*   **Given** a patron is using a cell phone at a high volume in the library
*   **When** the circulation staff identifies the disturbance
*   **Then** the staff follows the protocol to resolve the inappropriate noise level to maintain library standards.

**Scenario 16: Handling mismatched call numbers during shelving**
*   **Given** a book is being re-shelved
*   **When** the staff member finds the book does not match the Dewey Decimal or Library of Congress sequence on the shelf
*   **Then** the staff member must correct the placement to ensure the stack's integrity.
