### Happy Paths (Normal Operations)

**Scenario 1: Successful English Auction bid**
Given an ongoing English auction for a "Rare Painting" with a current bid of $1,000
When a bidder submits a higher bid of $1,100
Then the system should accept the bid and update the current highest price to $1,100.

**Scenario 2: Winning a No-Reserve auction**
Given a No-Reserve auction with several active bidders
When the auction ends and Bidder A has the highest bid of $500
Then the item must be sold to Bidder A regardless of the final price.

**Scenario 3: Successful Dutch Auction purchase**
Given a Dutch auction starting at $5,000 for a lot of tulips
When the auctioneer lowers the price to $4,000 and a bidder accepts
Then the auction ends immediately with the bidder paying $4,000.

**Scenario 4: First-Price Sealed-Bid winner determination**
Given a First-Price Sealed-Bid auction where three bidders submitted $100, $150, and $200 respectively
When the bids are opened after the deadline
Then the bidder who submitted $200 wins and pays exactly $200.

**Scenario 5: Vickrey Auction (Second-Price) payment**
Given a Vickrey auction where the highest bid is $500 and the second-highest bid is $450
When the auction concludes
Then the highest bidder wins the item but pays the second-highest price of $450.

### Precondition Violations

**Scenario 6: Rejecting a lower bid in an English Auction**
Given an English auction where the current highest bid is $2,000
When a bidder attempts to place a bid of $1,900
Then the system should reject the bid for not exceeding the current price.

**Scenario 7: Bid submitted after Candle Auction expiration**
Given a Candle Auction is in progress
When the candle flame expires and a bidder shouts a bid a second later
Then the auctioneer must reject the bid as the auction is officially closed.

**Scenario 8: Re-entry in a Japanese Auction**
Given a Japanese Auction where a participant has already dropped out
When the participant attempts to re-enter the bidding as the price increases
Then the system must deny the entry according to the "no re-entry" rule.

---

### State Transitions

**Scenario 9: Transition from Active to Sold (Reserve Met)**
Given a Reserve Auction with a reserve price of $1,000
When the bidding reaches $1,050 and the time expires
Then the item state changes from "Active" to "Sold".

**Scenario 10: Transition from Active to Unsold (Reserve Not Met)**
Given a Reserve Auction with a hidden reserve of $5,000
When the auction ends with a highest bid of $4,500
Then the item state changes to "Unsold" and the seller retains the item.

**Scenario 11: Japanese Auction price escalation**
Given an active Japanese Auction with multiple participants
When the price increments to the next level
Then all participants must signal their intent to continue or be moved to the "Dropped Out" state.

### Edge Cases and Boundary Conditions

**Scenario 12: Auction with only one bidder and one seller**
Given a forum where only one buyer and one seller are present
When the buyer makes an offer to the seller
Then the system should classify this as a "trade" or "negotiation" rather than an "auction".

**Scenario 13: Combinatorial Auction "All-or-Nothing" package**
Given a combinatorial auction for Item A and Item B
When a bidder places a package bid for both items
Then the system must ensure the bidder receives both items if they win, or nothing if the package is outbid.

**Scenario 14: Minimum bidding increment enforcement**
Given an English auction with a current bid of $100 and a minimum increment of $10
When a bidder attempts to bid $105
Then the bid should be rejected for failing to meet the minimum increment.

**Scenario 15: Empty bid pool in a No-Reserve Auction**
Given a No-Reserve auction that reaches its time limit
When no bids have been submitted
Then the item state remains "Unsold" and the auction closes.

### Query Operations

**Scenario 16: Checking Bidding History (Transparency)**
Given an ongoing Reverse Auction
When a supplier queries the current bidding status
Then the system provides real-time visibility of competing offers to ensure information transparency.

**Scenario 17: Verifying Highest Bid in English Auction**
Given an English auction with multiple electronic bids
When a potential bidder views the auction page
Then the system must display the current highest bid to all participants.

---

### Error Handling

**Scenario 18: Handling simultaneous identical bids in a Sealed-Bid auction**
Given a First-Price Sealed-Bid auction
When two bidders submit the exact same highest bid price
Then the system should trigger a tie-breaking protocol (e.g., first-come-first-served or a re-bid).

**Scenario 19: Handling seller withdrawal in an Absolute Auction**
Given an auction advertised as "Absolute/No-Reserve"
When the seller attempts to withdraw the item during the bidding process
Then the system should notify the seller of potential legal/territorial restrictions on such an action.

**Scenario 20: Recovery from interruption in a Candle Auction**
Given a Candle Auction using a physical candle
When the candle is accidentally extinguished by a draft rather than burning out
Then the auctioneer must follow the protocol for a "void" event or restart the timing process.
