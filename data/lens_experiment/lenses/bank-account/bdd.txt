### Happy Paths (Normal Operations)

**Scenario 1: Successful account opening**
*   **Given** a potential customer is at least 18 years old
*   **When** the customer applies for a standard deposit account with valid identification
*   **Then** the bank should create a new account with a unique account number and record the initial deposit.

**Scenario 2: Successful deposit of funds**
*   **Given** an existing active bank account
*   **When** the customer deposits a sum of money into the account
*   **Then** the bank should increase the account balance by that sum and record the transaction as a liability for the bank.

**Scenario 3: Successful withdrawal from a deposit account**
*   **Given** a deposit account has a positive credit balance of $500
*   **When** the customer requests a withdrawal of $200
*   **Then** the bank should provide the funds and update the account balance to $300.

**Scenario 4: Periodic bank statement generation**
*   **Given** several transactions have occurred on an account within the last month
*   **When** the monthly reporting cycle ends
*   **Then** the bank should generate a bank statement reporting all transactions and the final financial position.

---

### Precondition Violations (Invalid State)

**Scenario 5: Opening an account with a false name**
*   **Given** a customer provides identification documents that are verified as false
*   **When** the customer attempts to open a new bank account
*   **Then** the bank must reject the application as it is unlawful to open an account in a false name.

**Scenario 6: Underage account opening without guardian**
*   **Given** a minor is under the minimum age of 16/18 required by local law
*   **When** the minor attempts to open and operate an account independently
*   **Then** the system should prevent the account creation unless a parent or guardian is associated with the account.

**Scenario 7: Withdrawal exceeding available balance (No credit limit)**
*   **Given** a deposit account has a balance of $100 and no overdraft facility
*   **When** the customer attempts to withdraw $150
*   **Then** the transaction should be rejected due to insufficient funds.

---

### State Transitions

**Scenario 8: Account balance switches from Credit to Debit**
*   **Given** a flexible account has a positive balance of $50
*   **When** a transaction of $100 is processed (under an agreed credit limit)
*   **Then** the account state should transition from a "Deposit" nature (Credit) to a "Loan" nature (Debit).

**Scenario 9: EMI Account Onboarding Speed**
*   **Given** a business requires an account for international transactions
*   **When** the business applies for an Electronic Money Institution (EMI) account
*   **Then** the account setup state should transition to "Active" significantly faster than a traditional bank onboarding process.

---

### Edge Cases (Boundaries/Empty/Full)

**Scenario 10: Zero balance account status**
*   **Given** a new account is opened but no funds have been deposited yet
*   **When** the customer queries the account status
*   **Then** the account should be active but display a balance of zero.

**Scenario 11: Transaction at the exact withdrawal limit**
*   **Given** an account has a daily withdrawal limit of $1,000
*   **When** the customer attempts to withdraw exactly $1,000
*   **Then** the transaction should be approved and the remaining daily limit should be updated to $0.

---

### Query Operations (Checking State)

**Scenario 12: Checking financial position**
*   **Given** an account holder has multiple accounts including a loan and a savings account
*   **When** the customer requests their current financial position
*   **Then** the system should return the calculated balance across all identified account numbers.

**Scenario 13: Verifying EMI Safeguarding**
*   **Given** a customer holds funds in an EMI account
*   **When** the customer audits how their funds are held
*   **Then** the system should confirm the funds are safeguarded in a third-party credit institution and not used for lending.

---

### Error Handling

**Scenario 14: System failure during funds transfer**
*   **Given** a customer initiates a wire transfer between two accounts
*   **When** a technical communication error occurs mid-transaction
*   **Then** the system should ensure the balance is not deducted from the source account if it cannot be verified as reaching the destination.

**Scenario 15: Transaction blocked by monitoring**
*   **Given** a traditional bank account with strict transaction monitoring
*   **When** an unusual or high-risk transfer is attempted
*   **Then** the system should block the transaction and trigger a query to the account holder for verification.
