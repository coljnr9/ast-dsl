1. When RESET is TRUE, the CTUD shall set CV to 0.
2. While RESET is TRUE, the CTUD shall maintain CV at 0.
3. When LOAD is TRUE, the CTUD shall set CV to the value of PV.
4. While LOAD is TRUE, the CTUD shall maintain CV at the value of PV.
5. While RESET is TRUE, when LOAD is TRUE, the CTUD shall prioritize RESET and set CV to 0.
6. When a rising edge occurs on CU, the CTUD shall increment CV by one.
7. When a rising edge occurs on CD, the CTUD shall decrement CV by one.
8. While RESET is TRUE, when a rising edge occurs on CU, the CTUD shall keep CV at 0.
9. While LOAD is TRUE, when a rising edge occurs on CU, the CTUD shall keep CV at the value of PV.
10. While CV is greater than or equal to PV, the CTUD shall set QU to TRUE.
11. While CV is less than PV, the CTUD shall set QU to FALSE.
12. While CV is equal to 0, the CTUD shall set QD to TRUE.
13. While CV is not equal to 0, the CTUD shall set QD to FALSE.
14. While CV is at the maximum limit of the WORD data type, when a rising edge occurs on CU, the CTUD shall maintain CV at the maximum limit.
15. While CV is 0, when a rising edge occurs on CD, the CTUD shall maintain CV at 0.
16. While no rising edge is detected on CU and CD, and RESET and LOAD are FALSE, the CTUD shall preserve the current value of CV.
17. If a rising edge occurs on both CU and CD simultaneously, then the CTUD shall increment and then decrement CV, resulting in no net change.
18. When PV changes, the CTUD shall update the state of QU based on the current CV.
19. The CTUD shall use the WORD data type for the PV input.
20. The CTUD shall use the WORD data type for the CV output.
21. While RESET is FALSE and LOAD is FALSE, when a rising edge occurs on CU, the CTUD shall increment CV.
22. While RESET is FALSE and LOAD is FALSE, when a rising edge occurs on CD, the CTUD shall decrement CV.
23. The CTUD shall provide the current counter value via the CV output.
24. The CTUD shall provide the upper limit status via the QU output.
25. The CTUD shall provide the zero status via the QD output.
