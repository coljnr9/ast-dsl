### 2. System Preservation Requirements
Even when failures occur, the system must preserve:
*   **Privacy Integrity:** Under no circumstances should an "unlisted/private" number be leaked through search or reverse lookup.
*   **Alphabetical Integrity:** The sequence must be maintained to ensure the "Let your fingers do the walking" heuristic remains functional.
*   **Emergency Access:** Critical numbers (hospitals, emergency services) must remain accessible even if residential listings are deprecated.

---

### 3. Permanent Transitions (Irreversible States)
*   **Unlisted Status:** Once a subscriber pays/requests to be unlisted, the transition out of the public directory is effectively permanent for that publication cycle.
*   **Historical Archive:** Once a directory is "preserved by BT Archives" or similar bodies, its contents become a permanent historical record that cannot be updated or corrected.
*   **Transition to Digital:** Once a community ceases printing residential white pages (e.g., NY 2010), the system transitions to a "Digital-Only" state for that demographic.

---

### 4. Categorical Impossibilities
*   **Cellular Lookup:** In the US system, it is currently impossible to find mobile or VoIP numbers in the primary directory due to privacy/telemarketing regulations.
*   **Reverse Lookup in All Regions:** In certain jurisdictions, the "Grey Pages" functionality is natively disabled/impossible.
*   **Real-Time Update (Print):** A hard-copy directory cannot be updated once the 600,000 tons of paper have been printed and distributed.

---

### 5. Edge Cases
*   **The "Measles" Scenario (Operator Dependency):** Early historical systems relied on human memory/health. If operators are incapacitated, the "Directory" (as an interface) fails entirely until the transition to the "Parker Format" (alphabetical/numbered list).
*   **Commonality Overload:** A subscriber with a common name and no provided address makes the directory unusable for that specific entity discovery.
*   **Language Barrier:** Bilingual directories (e.g., Rafu Telephone Guide) create a failure mode where search fails if the user does not possess the secondary language's character set.

---

### 6. Out of Scope (Explicit Non-Support)
*   **Copyright Protection (US):** The system does **not** support intellectual property claims on the data itself (per *Feist v. Rural*).
*   **Real-Time Presence:** The directory does not indicate if a subscriber is currently "on the line" or available; it is a static registry, not a status indicator.
*   **Global Single-Source:** There is no single "Phone Book"; the system is strictly partitioned by geographical area or specific service provider.
