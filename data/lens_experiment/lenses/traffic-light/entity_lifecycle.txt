This domain analysis outlines the core entities, operations, and logic of a Traffic Signaling System based on the provided text.

### **ENTITIES**

**1. Signal Head**
*   **Data:** Orientation (Vertical/Horizontal), Mounting Type (e.g., Tower, Mast arm, Doghouse), Physical Shape (Circular, Arrow, Square, Diamond).
*   **Creation/Destruction:** Installed by authorities at intersections/crossings; decommissioned or replaced during technology upgrades.
*   **States:** 
    *   *Active:* Participating in traffic control.
    *   *Flashing/Preempted:* Overridden by emergency vehicles or rail level crossings.
    *   *Dark/Out of Order:* Power failure or maintenance required.

**2. Aspect (Lamp/Light)**
*   **Data:** Color (Red, Amber, Green, White, Blue), Symbol (Solid, Arrow, Walking Man, Hand, Bicycle, Countdown Number), Mode (Steady, Flashing, Rapidly Flashing).
*   **Lifecycle:** Modified by the Controller to change state (Illuminated vs. Extinguished).
*   **States:** `OFF`, `STEADY_ON`, `FLASHING`.

**3. Controller**
*   **Data:** Logic Type (Fixed Timer, Computerized, Volume-based/Sensor-driven), Phase Timing (Seconds), Communication Link (Telephone lines, Fiber).
*   **Lifecycle:** Created as a physical hardware unit; logic is modified via programming updates.
*   **States:** `MANUAL_CONTROL` (Police/Traffic Officer), `AUTOMATIC_TIMER`, `COMPUTER_COORDINATED`, `VOLUME_SENSITIVE`.

**4. Intersection / Crossing**
*   **Data:** Location, Road Type (Standard, Level Crossing, Tidal Flow, Smart Motorway), Participating Flows (Vehicular, Pedestrian, Rail, Aircraft).
*   **Relationships:** Contains multiple Signal Heads.

**5. Detection Sensor (Implied)**
*   **Data:** Vehicle presence, Pedestrian push-button activation.
*   **Lifecycle:** Actively triggers transitions in the Controller’s state.

---

### **OPERATIONS**

**1. Change Phase**
*   **Entities:** Controller, Signal Head, Aspects.
*   **Preconditions:** Current phase duration has expired OR a sensor has detected a vehicle/pedestrian demand.
*   **Changes:** Specific Aspects transition (e.g., Green `OFF` $\rightarrow$ Amber `STEADY_ON`).
*   **Stays Same:** Intersection geometry and physical signal head positions.

**2. Preempt Signal**
*   **Entities:** Controller, Emergency Vehicle/Train.
*   **Preconditions:** High-priority vehicle approaches or movable barrier closes (runway/bridge).
*   **Changes:** Normal cycle is interrupted; specific aspects (usually Red) are forced on to clear the path.

**3. Display Countdown**
*   **Entities:** Countdown Light (Aspect).
*   **Preconditions:** Main Signal is in a timed state.
*   **Changes:** Numerical value decrements per second until reaching zero.

**4. Pedestrian Request**
*   **Entities:** Pedestrian, Push Button (Sensor), Controller.
*   **Preconditions:** System is in a vehicular-priority state.
*   **Changes:** Controller queues a "Walk" phase transition.

---

### **RELATIONSHIPS**

*   **Ownership (Composition):** An **Intersection** owns multiple **Signal Heads**. A **Signal Head** contains one or more **Aspects**.
*   **Control (Reference):** A **Controller** references and manages the state of multiple **Signal Heads** to prevent conflicting traffic flows.
*   **Dependency:** **Pedestrian Signals** are often linked to **Vehicular Signals** (e.g., "Parallel Walk" where the green man coincides with the green car light).
*   **Indexing:** Signal heads are indexed by **Direction** (Northbound, Left-turn filter) and **User Type** (Bus, Bicycle, Pedestrian).

---

### **INVARIANTS**

1.  **Conflict Avoidance:** No two conflicting traffic flows (e.g., North-South Green and East-West Green) may be active simultaneously.
2.  **Sequence Integrity:** Vehicular signals must follow a standardized sequence (e.g., Green $\rightarrow$ Amber $\rightarrow$ Red) to prevent sudden stops and "Yellow Traps."
3.  **The "Red Rule":** During a "Red/Amber" transition (UK/Germany), the legal requirement to remain stopped (Red rule) applies until the Green aspect is shown.
4.  **Fail-Safe:** If a system fails or enters a "Dilemma Zone," it should default to a safe state (e.g., Flashing Amber/Red or All-Red).
5.  **Visibility:** Signal aspects must be arranged in a specific order (Red on top/left) to ensure accessibility for color-blind users.
