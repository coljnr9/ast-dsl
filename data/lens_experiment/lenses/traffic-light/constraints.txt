1. A standard vertical signal head must arrange colors from top to bottom as red, amber, and then green.
2. A standard horizontal signal head must arrange colors from left to right as red, amber, and then green.
3. Drivers must not proceed beyond the stop line or enter an intersection when a non-flashing red light is displayed.
4. Drivers must not pass the stop line when an amber light is displayed unless they cannot stop safely.
5. Drivers may proceed when a green light is displayed provided the intersection is clear and can be exited before the next phase change.
6. A red and amber light displayed simultaneously indicates the signal is about to change to green but the requirement to stop remains in effect.
7. A flashing red light requires traffic to stop before the intersection and then proceed according to standard stop sign rules.
8. A flashing amber light indicates traffic may proceed through the intersection with caution.
9. A green arrow allows traffic to proceed only in the specific direction indicated by the arrow.
10. A green arrow displayed with a red light grants priority to turning traffic over oncoming traffic.
11. A flashing amber arrow requires drivers to give way to other vehicles and pedestrians before turning in the direction of the arrow.
12. A red arrow forbids traffic from making the specific movement indicated by the arrow.
13. Pedestrians may only begin crossing a roadway when a "walk" symbol (green or white walking figure) is displayed.
14. Pedestrians must not start to cross the road when a flashing red or orange "don't walk" symbol appears.
15. Pedestrians already in the roadway when a "don't walk" signal begins flashing must continue to a place of safety.
16. Pedestrians are forbidden from entering the intersection when a steady red or orange "don't walk" symbol is displayed.
17. A countdown light must reach zero or one at the exact moment the main signal color changes.
18. In South Korea, traffic is prohibited from entering an intersection on amber even if a safe stop is not possible.
19. Transitions between signal phases must avoid the "yellow trap" where turning drivers mistakenly assume oncoming traffic has been signaled to stop.
20. In the United Kingdom, vehicles are strictly prohibited from blocking an intersection or crossing even when the signal is green.
21. Automated timers may be used to transition signal states without manual human intervention.
22. Vehicle detection sensors may be used by the control system to trigger phase changes based on traffic volume.
23. Emergency vehicle preemption may override normal signal cycles to grant priority to first responders.
24. At a pedestrian scramble (all-red stage), all vehicular traffic must stop to allow pedestrians to cross in any direction, including diagonally.
25. HAWK beacons must remain unlit until a pedestrian activates the system via a push button.
26. A leading pedestrian interval must provide a "walk" signal to pedestrians shortly before the parallel vehicular traffic receives a green light.
27. Traffic signals at railway level crossings or low-flying aircraft approaches may use flashing red lights combined with sirens or voice prompts.
28. Speed signs may display recommended speeds to allow drivers to reach the next intersection during a green phase.
29. In "parallel walk" configurations, turning vehicles must yield to pedestrians crossing in the same direction of travel.
30. The system does not support the withdrawal of a pedestrian crossing request once the push button has been activated.
