Here are the behavioral requirements for the Traffic Light system (referred to as the "Stoplight" or "Signal-head") based on the provided article, using EARS notation:

**Normal Operations (Ubiquitous)**
1. The Stoplight shall transmit information to road users through colors and symbols including arrows and bicycles.
2. The Stoplight shall arrange the red, amber, and green signals either vertically with red on top or horizontally.
3. The Stoplight shall provide non-flashing red, amber, and green lights for standard three-color signal heads.
4. The Stoplight shall display a green walking human figure to indicate a safe-to-cross state for pedestrians.
5. The Stoplight shall display a red or orange standing man or hand symbol to indicate a do-not-walk state for pedestrians.

**State-Driven Behavior (State-driven)**
6. While in a non-flashing Red state, the Stoplight shall prohibit traffic from proceeding beyond the stop line.
7. While in a Green state, the Stoplight shall allow traffic to proceed through the intersection.
8. While in an Amber state, the Stoplight shall indicate that the green movement is being terminated.
9. While the main light is red, the Stoplight shall display a Green Arrow to permit traffic to proceed in a specific direction.
10. While transitioning from green to red, the countdown light shall display the remaining seconds in a color matching the active signal.

**Event-Driven Behavior (Event-driven)**
11. When the red-amber sequence is initiated (UK standard), the Stoplight shall signal drivers to prepare to proceed without entering the intersection.
12. When a Green Arrow is combined with a green signal, the Stoplight shall indicate that turning traffic has priority over oncoming traffic.
13. When the countdown reaches zero, the Stoplight shall immediately change the main light color to the next phase.
14. When a pedestrian pushes the cross button (HAWK beacon), the Stoplight shall initiate an amber light followed by red lights.
15. When an aircraft is landing or taking off at a runway crossing, the Stoplight shall close the movable barrier.

**Unwanted/Error/Rejection Cases (Unwanted)**
16. If the Stoplight system fails or is out of order, then the Stoplight shall flash amber to indicate road users must proceed with caution.
17. If a "yellow trap" condition exists, then the Stoplight shall display a sign reading "Oncoming traffic has extended green."
18. If a driver cannot safely stop when the amber light appears (except in South Korea), then the Stoplight shall allow the vehicle to pass the stop line.
19. If a vehicle would not clear the intersection before the next phase change, then the Stoplight shall prohibit the vehicle from proceeding on green.

**Complex/Specific Conditions (Complex)**
20. While at a level crossing or swing bridge, when a flashing red light is displayed, the Stoplight shall activate additional alarms and voice prompts.
21. While in a flashing amber arrow state, the Stoplight shall indicate that drivers must give way to other users before turning.
22. While the red signal is active, when a circular red light flashes (US/MUTCD), the Stoplight shall allow traffic to proceed after a full stop as per STOP sign rules.
23. While in the "advance green" state (Ontario), when the green lamp flashes rapidly, the Stoplight shall permit straight-through movement and protected left turns.
24. While the countdown timer exceeds 99 seconds, the Stoplight shall display a letter (e.g., A0) or a flashing "99" to indicate the extended duration.
25. While in the "all-red" stage (Pedestrian Scramble), the Stoplight shall hold all vehicular traffic to allow pedestrians to cross diagonally.

**Scope and Preservation (Ubiquitous/State-driven)**
26. The Stoplight shall not recognize a "dilemma zone" for amber lights when operating under South Korean legal jurisdiction.
27. The Stoplight shall maintain the Red signal rule while the Red/Amber transition signal is active.
28. The Stoplight shall remain unlit (HAWK beacon) until a pedestrian trigger is received.
29. The Stoplight shall not allow the display of a circular green signal in a flashing mode.
30. The Stoplight shall preserve the square shape for red signals and diamond shape for amber signals when deployed in Quebec.
