Traffic lights are automated signaling devices designed to regulate competing flows of vehicles and pedestrians at intersections and crossings. Their core purpose is to improve road safety and intersection capacity by alternating the right-of-way between different directions of travel. While primarily used for vehicular traffic, these systems also incorporate specialized signals for pedestrians, cyclists, and public transit, and are increasingly integrated into smart motorway and railway systems.

The system operates using signal heads—arrays of colored lights known as aspects—that transmit instructions through standardized colors and symbols. The universal sequence consists of red (stop), amber or yellow (caution/transition), and green (proceed). Operations are typically managed by computerized controllers that follow fixed timers or respond to real-time traffic volume. Key entities include vehicle signals, which may include supplemental green "filter" arrows for protected turns, and pedestrian signals, which use "walk" and "don't walk" icons to coordinate safe crossing intervals. Relationships between these entities are defined by phases; for example, an "all-red" phase may stop all vehicular traffic to allow a "pedestrian scramble," where people cross in all directions simultaneously.

The system is governed by strict behavioral rules and technical constraints to prevent collisions. A primary rule is that a red light constitutes a mandatory stop, though some jurisdictions allow turns after stopping if no conflicting traffic is present. The amber phase serves as a critical buffer, requiring drivers to stop unless they are too close to the intersection to do so safely. Important edge cases include "yellow traps," where a driver turning across traffic mistakenly assumes oncoming vehicles have a red light, and "dilemma zones," where the timing of the yellow light makes it difficult to decide whether to stop or proceed. Some regions also utilize countdown timers or flashing green lights to provide additional telemetry to road users regarding upcoming signal changes.
