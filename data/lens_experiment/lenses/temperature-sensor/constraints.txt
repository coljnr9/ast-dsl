As a domain expert on the DS18B20 1-Wire Digital Thermometer, I have extracted the following behavioral rules, restrictions, and constraints:

1. The device must communicate over a 1-Wire bus requiring only one data line (DQ) and ground (GND).
2. The device can derive power either from an external supply on the VDD pin or directly from the DQ data line via "parasite power."
3. When operating in parasite power mode, the VDD pin must be connected to ground.
4. Each device must be uniquely identified by a factory-lasered 64-bit ROM code consisting of an 8-bit family code (28h), a 48-bit serial number, and an 8-bit CRC.
5. The device supports a user-programmable temperature-to-digital resolution of 9, 10, 11, or 12 bits.
6. The temperature sensor measures from -55°C to +125°C.
7. At power-up, the device defaults to a 12-bit resolution and a low-power idle state.
8. The digital output of a temperature conversion is stored as a 16-bit sign-extended two’s complement number.
9. An alarm flag is set if a measured temperature is lower than or equal to the TL register or higher than or equal to the TH register.
10. The alarm flag is updated after every temperature measurement and will clear if the alarm condition no longer exists after a subsequent conversion.
11. The TH, TL, and Configuration registers must be nonvolatile (EEPROM) to retain data during power-down.
12. A temperature conversion is initiated only when the master issues a Convert T [44h] command.
13. During a temperature conversion or a copy-to-EEPROM operation in parasite power mode, the master must provide a strong pullup on the bus within 10μs of the command.
14. No other bus activity is permitted while a strong pullup is enabled during a conversion or EEPROM write.
15. Externally powered devices must signal a "0" during conversion if a read time slot is issued and a "1" when the conversion is complete.
16. Parasite power is not recommended for applications where temperatures exceed +100°C due to increased leakage currents.
17. Every transaction sequence must follow a strict order: Initialization, followed by a ROM Command, followed by a Function Command.
18. Initialization must consist of a reset pulse from the master followed by a presence pulse from the slave(s).
19. If the bus is held low for more than 480μs, all slave devices on the bus will perform a reset.
20. All data and commands must be transmitted least significant bit (LSB) first.
21. The master must use the Match ROM [55h] command to address a specific device in a multidrop environment.
22. The Skip ROM [CCh] command allows the master to address all devices simultaneously, but a Read Scratchpad command cannot follow it if multiple devices are on the bus.
23. The Alarm Search [ECh] command limits responses to only those devices that have a set alarm flag.
24. When using the Write Scratchpad [4Eh] command, the master must write all three bytes (TH, TL, and Config) before issuing a reset to avoid data corruption.
25. Data is transferred from the scratchpad to the EEPROM only upon receipt of the Copy Scratchpad [48h] command.
26. EEPROM data is automatically reloaded into the scratchpad registers upon power-up.
27. The master can manually trigger a data reload from EEPROM to the scratchpad using the Recall E2 [B8h] command.
28. To verify data integrity during a read, the master must calculate the CRC of the received data and compare it to the CRC byte transmitted by the device.
29. The device determines its power mode (parasite vs. external) and informs the master when it receives a Read Power Supply [B4h] command.
30. Write and read time slots must be a minimum of 60μs in duration with at least 1μs of recovery time between slots.
