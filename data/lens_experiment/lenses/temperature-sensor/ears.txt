Here are the behavioral requirements for the DS18B20 Digital Thermometer using EARS notation:

**Normal Operations & Ubiquitous Requirements**
1. The DS18B20 shall provide 9-bit to 12-bit Celsius temperature measurements.
2. The DS18B20 shall communicate with a central microprocessor over a 1-Wire bus.
3. The DS18B20 shall store a unique 64-bit serial code in on-board ROM.
4. The DS18B20 shall follow the 1-Wire bus protocol for all data and command transmissions.
5. The DS18B20 shall store temperature data as a 16-bit sign-extended two’s complement number.

**State-Driven Behavior**
6. While in the power-up state, the DS18B20 shall default to 12-bit resolution.
7. While in the power-up state, the DS18B20 shall load 85°C into the temperature register.
8. While in the power-up state, the DS18B20 shall automatically reload TH, TL, and configuration data from EEPROM to the scratchpad.
9. While performing a temperature conversion, the DS18B20 shall return a "0" response to read time slots if powered by an external VDD.
10. While a temperature conversion is complete, the DS18B20 shall return a "1" response to read time slots if powered by an external VDD.

**Event-Driven Behavior**
11. When the master issues a Convert T [44h] command, the DS18B20 shall initiate a temperature-to-digital conversion.
12. When a temperature conversion is completed, the DS18B20 shall store the thermal data in the 2-byte temperature register.
13. When a temperature conversion is completed, the DS18B20 shall return to a low-power idle state.
14. When the master issues an Alarm Search [ECh] command, the DS18B20 shall respond only if the internal alarm flag is set.
15. When the master issues a Write Scratchpad [4Eh] command, the DS18B20 shall update the TH, TL, and configuration registers.
16. When the master issues a Copy Scratchpad [48h] command, the DS18B20 shall transfer TH, TL, and configuration data from SRAM to EEPROM.
17. When the master issues a Read Power Supply [B4h] command, the DS18B20 shall pull the bus low if operating in parasite power mode.
18. When the DS18B20 detects a rising edge after a reset pulse, the DS18B20 shall transmit a presence pulse after 15μs to 60μs.

**Unwanted Behavior (Error & Rejection)**
19. If the 1-Wire bus is held low for more than 480μs, then the DS18B20 shall perform a reset of all internal components.
20. If the measured temperature is ≥ TH or ≤ TL, then the DS18B20 shall set the internal alarm flag.
21. If the measured temperature returns to the programmed limits, then the DS18B20 shall clear the alarm flag after the next conversion.
22. If the voltage on any pin is outside the range of -0.5V to +6.0V, then the DS18B20 shall consider this an absolute maximum rating violation.

**Complex Requirements**
23. While in parasite power mode, when the master issues a Convert T [44h] command, the DS18B20 shall derive operating current from the high-signaled DQ pin via an internal capacitor.
24. While in parasite power mode, when the master performs a temperature conversion or EEPROM write, the DS18B20 shall require the bus to be held high by a strong pullup within 10μs.

**Preservation & Scope Boundaries**
25. The DS18B20 shall retain data in the TH, TL, and configuration registers during power-down using nonvolatile EEPROM.
26. The DS18B20 shall preserve the 64-bit unique serial code as a read-only value in ROM.
27. The DS18B20 shall not prevent command sequences from proceeding if the bus master's calculated CRC does not match the device CRC.
28. The DS18B20 shall not perform Fahrenheit conversions internally.
