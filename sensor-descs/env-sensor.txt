Write a domain model of an environmental sensor (inspired by the BME280) that measures temperature, pressure, and humidity. The sensor has three operating modes: sleep (no measurements, lowest power), forced (performs a single measurement then returns to sleep), and normal (continuously cycles between measurement and idle periods). Each of the three channels (temperature, pressure, humidity) has an independently configurable oversampling setting that controls measurement precision — valid settings are off, 1x, 2x, 4x, 8x, and 16x. When a channel's oversampling is set to off, that channel is skipped during measurement. The sensor also has a configurable IIR filter coefficient that smooths pressure and temperature readings. Measurements can only be read after at least one measurement cycle has completed; reading before that is undefined. A soft reset returns the sensor to its power-on default state (sleep mode, all oversampling at 1x, filter off).
