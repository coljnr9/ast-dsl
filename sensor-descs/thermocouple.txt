Write a domain model of a thermocouple interface (inspired by the MAX31855). The device reads temperature from an external thermocouple probe and also measures its own internal cold-junction temperature for compensation. Both readings are available after initialization. The device monitors three fault conditions: open circuit (thermocouple disconnected), short to ground, and short to VCC. Each fault is reported as an independent boolean flag. When any fault is active, the thermocouple temperature reading becomes undefined, but the cold-junction reading remains valid. Faults are latched — once detected, they persist until explicitly cleared. After clearing, the device re-evaluates the thermocouple connection on the next read cycle.
