Write a domain model of a multi-channel analog-to-digital converter (inspired by the ADS1115). The device has four input channels, selectable by a channel index. A programmable gain amplifier (PGA) with six gain settings controls the input voltage range. The ADC operates in two modes: single-shot (performs one conversion then enters a low-power idle state) and continuous (repeatedly converts at a configurable data rate). In single-shot mode, a conversion must be explicitly triggered, and the result is available only after conversion completes. In continuous mode, the latest conversion result is always available once the first conversion finishes. The device also includes a comparator that monitors the conversion result against configurable high and low thresholds. The comparator can operate in traditional mode (asserts when the reading exceeds the high threshold, de-asserts when it falls below the low threshold) or window mode (asserts when the reading is outside the window defined by both thresholds).
