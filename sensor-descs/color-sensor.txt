Write a domain model of an RGBC color sensor (inspired by the TCS34725). The sensor measures light intensity across four channels: red, green, blue, and clear (unfiltered). The sensor can be enabled or disabled; when disabled, no measurements are taken and readings are undefined. Integration time is configurable and determines how long the sensor accumulates light per measurement — longer integration times give higher resolution but slower updates. Gain is also configurable with four levels (1x, 4x, 16x, 60x) to handle different lighting conditions. After the sensor is enabled and at least one integration cycle completes, the four channel readings become available. The sensor also supports an interrupt mechanism: a high and low threshold can be set on the clear channel, and a persistence filter configures how many consecutive out-of-range readings are required before the interrupt fires. The interrupt flag can be read and explicitly cleared.
